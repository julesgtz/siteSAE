The History of Rap

Where does the rap come from?

Rap is a musical style named after rhythm and poetry. This style of music appeared in the mid-1970s in the ghetto of the United States and belongs to the cultural movement of hip-hop. At that time, there were big, free, forbidden parties organised in the streets or in cellars. These parties were called block parties, and the masters of ceremonies, who later became known as rappers, took advantage of the opportunity to rhyme while the music played. This is how rap started.

A different style

The particularity of this style of music lies in its half-spoken, half-sung style of diction and its many sound effects. Rhymes took over urban music and the streets became the temples of a style that sought to convey in a poetic and artistic way what was happening in an environment full of violence where the struggle against the system was constant.

The pioneers of rap

The first MC was Coke La Rock (MC means "master of ceremonies"), an American who is considered the first rapper and therefore the precursor of rap. Tupac Shakur (2pak), a rapper, probably the most famous, focused his songs mainly on social issues such as racism and drugs. There was also Notorius B.I.G. with his 30 million albums sold...
